North East

Northeast India is a land of misty mountains, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant culture. Imagine walking through the lively streets of Guwahati, exploring the serene monasteries of Tawang, or witnessing the breathtaking views of Dirang and Bomdila. North East Travel Guide

Welcome to North East India

The North East is a region of rolling hills, snow-capped peaks, and vibrant cultures. From the lively streets of Guwahati to the misty valleys of Shillong and the serene monasteries of Tawang, every destination here is filled with charm. Nature, tradition, and adventure come together to make the North East a traveler’s paradise.

Shillong – Scotland of the East

Known as the “Scotland of the East,” Shillong is famous for its rolling hills, waterfalls, and colonial charm. Explore Umiam Lake, Elephant Falls, and the bustling Police Bazaar. Shillong’s music culture and cool climate make it one of the most charming hill stations in India.

Guwahati – Gateway to the North East

Guwahati, Assam’s largest city, is both spiritual and modern. Visit the sacred Kamakhya Temple, take a cruise on the Brahmaputra, or explore Assam State Zoo and local markets. Guwahati is the perfect starting point for exploring the entire region.

Tawang – Monasteries & Mountains

Tawang is a serene Himalayan destination, home to the famous Tawang Monastery. Surrounded by snow peaks and high-altitude lakes, it’s a blend of natural beauty and spirituality. The journey to Tawang through winding mountain passes is as breathtaking as the destination itself.
